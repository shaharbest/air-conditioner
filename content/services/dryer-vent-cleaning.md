Clogged dryer vents are the leading cause of residential fires in the United States. The U.S. Fire Administration reports nearly 3,000 dryer fires per year — and lint buildup is the culprit in the majority of cases.
<!--more-->

A clean dryer vent doesn't just protect your home — it also cuts drying time, reduces energy use, and extends the life of your dryer.

## What We Do

We use professional-grade rotary brush systems and high-powered vacuums to clear lint, debris, and blockages from your entire dryer vent line — from the dryer connection to the exterior exhaust cap.

## Signs Your Dryer Vent Needs Cleaning

- Clothes take more than one cycle to dry
- Dryer feels hot to the touch during operation
- Burning smell when the dryer runs
- Exterior vent flap doesn't open when dryer is on
- It's been more than a year since your last cleaning

## How Often Should You Clean It?

At least once a year for a typical household. More frequently if you do heavy laundry loads or have long vent runs.
